From President's Pen ...

Last Sunday, our youth took the front stage to sing Bhajans at the Center, and what a treat it was! As always, they presented a very consecrated show of bhajans with superb melody. A big thank-you to you all.

Also, the tribute to Mr. S. Rao was very well attended. Mr. Rao has left behind a legacy of simple living and high thinking that will remain with us for years to come.

We are invited to sing bhajans at the Hindu Mandir, St. Albert Trail, this coming Sunday, March 15th, from 10:30am-11:30am; in case you wish to be a lead singer, please get in touch with Troy right away. Otherwise, please join the party at 10:30am at the temple on St. Albert Trail.

We are always looking for new Seva projects; please contact our unsung hero, Anshuman Tyagi for guidelines and help availability.

At the City Hall, our SSE students, youth members, and other dedicated volunteers have prepared a very informative display of Sai Values of multi-faith as an out reach project. Great efforts and imagination was put into making these exhibits and do deserve your appreciation. This exhibit will stay on until the end of this month. Please try to visit this display of Sai values in pictorial form and encourage the enthusiastic architects.

Now that Covid-19 has been reported in Edmonton, It is natural that we would worry about new threats to our health. Please get up to date information from reliable sources such as Alberta Health (www.alberta.ca/coronavirus) and Alberta Health Services (www.ahs.ca/covid). Please take the preventive steps like hand washing regularly, coughing and sneezing in your sleeve, and not coming too close to other people to reduce your risk of getting infection. More importantly, in case you have flu like symptoms, please refrain yourself from public gatherings including at the Sai Center for your own wellbeing and of fellow devotees. Let us fight this threat together and nip the problem in the bud.
