From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

My humble Pranams to our beloved Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba. First, please join me in welcoming Aunty Shubra and Sister Shilpi who recently returned from India. We are all very happy to see you both back and doing well.

Second, "One without power does not mean lack of physical or mental strength." Baba

In my journey through life, there were so many opportunities for me to be a part of something or the other but I always had this nagging voice telling me, "what can I do to make a difference? Or, my input is insignificant, my help is not needed or there are enough volunteers." In hindsight, I felt that these thoughts kept me from getting involved and being a part of something. Taking to heart Swami's saying above, getting involved, in whatever way or however limited I thought my capacity was, or at a minimum, just being there, has given me the chance to serve and learn so many needful qualities that compliments my physical and mental strengths and abilities.

Take for instance, bhajan singing, I always enjoy music, whether it's film song, devotional songs or western music. When I heard Sai bhajans being sung for the first time, I thought wow, when am I going to sing like that or would I even have a chance to sing. All it truly took was effort on my part to come, sit and be a part of a practice session and someone to ask "hey do you sing bhajans?" or "do you know any bhajan?" It was all I really wanted or needed, and from that moment on, my love and interest in bhajan singing grew and became a strength.

On a daily basis I see these acts of kindness, compassion and support being displayed in our Center. Everyone, from seniors, middle aged, youths to SSSE students, who are helping or assisting in some way or the other. It truly warms my heart ❤ and I would really like to express my gratitude and appreciation to everyone

For those who are not sure when or how to get involved,my suggestion, get off the sidelines, jump in, get involved, share your strengths and abilities no matter how insignificant you feel they are. You will evolve stronger and wiser and you never know, you might even inspire someone else to action.

Have a wonderful week. Sairam,

Troy
