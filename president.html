From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

In last week’s newsletter, in the excitement of talking about the delightful and blissful experience that we all had during this year’s dear Swami’s birthday celebrations, I overlooked mentioning about a very notable service that our SSE students performed over that week: they undertook a bottle drive as a seva project and received empty bottles etc. worth about $400! I think that was an outstanding achievement. And, it is worth repeating that SSE students are preparing and serving lunch for the Operation Friendship Seniors Society as a monthly seva project. These students are practicing dear Swami’s teachings in a true sense.

Swami says, "The wise ones use money, strength, intelligence, skills, aptitudes and opportunities for helping others and making their lives happier. Thus, they win divine grace. For sevā (selfless service) is the highest form of worship." SSS 18.23: November 21, 1985

By now, you might have noticed in this newsletter the artwork by SSE Group 3 on the Theme: Nurture Mother Nature which was their project for Swami’s birthday. These are being reproduced here over the past week. Congratulations to the SSE students for undertaking these noble projects and to the SSE gurus for motivating the SSE children for these timely ideas.

Events related to Swami’s birthday are still happening. SSSIO is inviting everyone to join a special webinar featuring renowned speaker, Brother Satyajit Salian from Mumbai, India, this Saturday evening, December, 4th from 6:30pm Alberta time. I am sure you would not want to miss this rare opportunity to listen to an ardent devotee of Swami, who has served swami in different roles over the past many years. You can participate in the webinar which will be livestreamed on YouTube Channel of SSSIO Canada at https://youtu.be/qB_dOftXIOQ.

One of the very exciting news that I would like to share with you is that our Centre Library is opening this Sunday, Dec 5th, 2021 after three or more years of painstaking work by several volunteers led by Sister Yvonne Scherban. Please see further details below in this newsletter. Also, we have received a few new diaries and a number of Sai table and wall calendars for the year 2022 from Prasanthi Nilayam. The Centre cost was: Diaries $10.50; Table Calendar $1.50, and Wall Calendar $4.00. These will be on display at the Centre on Sunday, soon after the Satsang, and will be available for distribution. In case you would like to reserve a copy, please email me at  saicenteredmonton@telus.net

On an extremely sad not, with a heavy heart, I would like to inform you that Sister Devi George, younger sister of Kamla Pillay (T.R. Pillay), left for heavenly abode this past Sunday, November 28th in Edmonton. A true devotee of Swami, and a very skillful teacher, she served Swami at our Sai Centre as Bal Vikas (SSE) teacher for several years in mid-eighties. On behalf of the Sri Sathya Sai Baba Centre of Edmonton, I convey our heartfelt condolences to the bereaved family in Edmonton and South Africa. Please see Sister Devi George’s obituary in this newsletter.

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
