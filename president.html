From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

The melodious lyrics of Mahishasura Mardini Stotram are still humming in my mind. Yes, I am referring to the superb and elaborate Navaratri function that was held last Friday at the Centre. It was an excellent expression of devotion and prayers to Durga Maa, done in a very fitting way. Many thanks to our ladies’ group for organizing and successfully carrying out such an involved and blissful function. The worship also involved Kanya Pooja (worshiping young girls), who are the purest form of female energy that later grows into motherhood.

Dear Swami has enlightened us on the significance of worshiping the mother during Navaratri. In one of the discourses, Swami elucidated, "Recognizing one’s mother as the very embodiment of all divine forces, one must show reverence to her and treat her with love.This is the true message that the Navaratri gives us. She provides us energy, wealth, and intelligence. She represents all the three Goddesses that we worship during Navaratri.” (Divine Discourse October 14, 1988).

In a very pleasant coincidence, Thanksgiving was also celebrated last weekend. So, Happy Thanksgiving to all of you. I hope you all had a very enjoyable celebration, thanking the Lord for plentiful food, blissful time, and real gratification.

Avatar Declaration Day, the Day dear Swami revealed his supreme nature and declared his real mission of taking avatar, is on October 20th. To prepare us for this occasion, SSSIO Scriptural Studies Committee has prepared a study guide. We started to study this guide in our study circles on Wednesday evenings from 7:30-8.30pm, and are contemplating on ‘Faith and Surrender’, the topic of the first chapter. Swami says: When you have full faith, God will surely help you and make you fearless. People who have faith in God will never be let down. But those who have no faith, suffer. Please join us in the study circle on Wednesdays.

Winter is around the corner! Warm winter socks are being sought to support the seniors at Operation Friendship Senior Society (OFSS). You may drop off socks at the Center on Sundays during regular satsangh time. These will be collected and delivered to OFSS early November. Please look for the details of this and other service projects in this newsletter.

I must thank all those who volunteered to help clean the Centre after the big function last Friday, and am also grateful to all those who come regularly on Tuesdays. Offering selfless service at the lotus feet of Bhagwan, is the purest form of reverence that one could offer to Him.

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
