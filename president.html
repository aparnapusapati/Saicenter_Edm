From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

September is a very exciting time of the year as the new school year commences and students look forward to meeting and greeting their friends after a long break. SSE students are no exception. A warm welcome to all the students in the fresh school year; their first class is starting this Sunday, September 12th. May Goddess Saraswathi grant knowledge and wisdom to all the students and gurus for their commitment and dedication. Sister Jaya at the helm as the newly appointed SSE coordinator, has taken the bull by the horn and with the help of sister Rehana Bennett steering the SSE program in the right direction; my best wishes for a successful sail.

Offering meals to the homeless is back again. As we did it very successfully last year, we are sponsoring meals prepared by Padmanadi restaurant. The meals will be packaged and distributed to the needy at the Hope Mission on September 11th. The target for this year is to sponsor 100 meals at $10 per meal. It is a good opportunity to participate in a very noble service; please contact Brother Anshuman if you would like to participate at anshumantyagi@hotmail.com or call at  613-293-6530.

September is also the start of several Hindu auspicious festivals. My joyous greetings to all of you on this pious Ganesha Chaturthi on Friday, September 10th. The Centre has arranged a devout program for that day: in the morning, there will be paduka puja starting at 8am, and in the evening, starting at 6pm, Lord Ganesha Abhishek will be performed with the recitation of Sri Rudram. A delicious meal will be served as prasadam after the evening program. Please participate in the program and receive the divine bliss.

You are welcome to join the study circle every Wednesday at 7:30pm. At present, we are studying dear Swami’s discourses on a broad topic of ‘Strive For Unity, Purity and Divinity’. Swami says, 'Sath-Chith-Ananda (being-awareness-bliss) are the very nature of man. But man has forgotten his very nature and is wasting his time in the vain pursuit of fleeting pleasures. He is not able to realise the value of his innate nature, Sath-Chith-Ananda. Once he realises its value and experiences it, he can reach any exalted state. In fact, he becomes verily God.' From Swami’s Discourse on New Year Day 2003, Prasanthi Nilayam, 1 January 2003

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
