From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

This coming weekend, we will be celebrating Diwali, one of the most glamorous festivals in Hinduism. My hearty best wishes to you all on this pious occasion. Diwali is celebrated to commemorate the return of Lord Rama after 14 years of exile, after killing the demon king Ravana. Ravan was a great scholar, who had learned all major Vedas and Shastras, but that knowledge was of no use as in practical life he had the demonic qualities of lust, and ego which brought his downfall. Yet, when he was lying on the ground fatally injured by Lord Rama’s arrow, Rama asked His younger brother Laxman to go and get some scholarly wisdom from this scholar.

Dear Swami has narrated that incident in one of His discourses and said, "Realizing that Ravana would pass away in a few minutes, many sages, scholars, and noblemen came to Ravana and asked him, O Ravana! You performed many good deeds as well as evil deeds. What is it the most memorable thing that you have discovered in your life?"

Ravana replied, "Sirs! Don't delay the noble acts you want to perform at any time. Put into action whatever noble thoughts and feelings that arise in your heart instantaneously. Procrastination is the thief of time. It is dangerous to delay. Translate into action whatever good resolve emanates in your mind. We waste our time in postponing the good deeds. We should be ever ready to put into action all the good thoughts that arise in the mind." (Date: 21 May 2002)

So, on this auspicious occasion of Diwali, let us not delay doing our good deeds of service to others and singing the glory of the Lord. Centre is providing several opportunities to serve the society detailed in this newsletter. Also, we have Akhand Bhajans on the day of Diwali, November 12th from 9 am to 3 pm. After listening to some excellent bhajans recently from our members at the Centre, I can hardly wait to participate in this once-a-year opportunity.

Swami’s Birthday program has been announced in this newsletter, which essentially starts from the Akhand Bhajan on November 12th, the SSE program and Young Adult play on the 19th, and also when the Ladies will perform a special Service and then with the grand finale on the 23rd, with Paduka Pooja in the morning and offering of our prayers in the evening. So, please join in these celebrations with family and invite friends as well. Finally, I would like to remind what Swami has conveyed through His discourses on Ramayana and its teachings, “Hatred is the cause of war, and desires are the cause of hatred. Anger arises if desires are not fulfilled; Ego arises if desires are fulfilled. Hence, an effort should be made to cut down one’s desires, give up one’s hatred, and strive for Divine Grace.” (May 27, 1996) We can relate this very well to what is happening in various parts of the world today; it is devastating to note innocent people are being victims of this hatred. So, please let us pray for the global peace.

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
