From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram. On this Sunday, July 5th, we will be celebrating Guru Poornima. In the Hindu religion, there is great significance of a Guru:

Mother is considered our first Guru who brings us to the world; school teacher is the next who prepares us how to live in the world and finally our spiritual Guru, Who leads us to salvation from this world. From the day one, we are dependent upon our Guru, in one form or the other, to complete our life journey.

There is a famous verse from the poetry (Hindi) of Saint Kabir (1440 AD) who is revered equally by Hindus, Muslims, and Sikhs: “Guru Govind dono khade, Kaake laagu paaye Balihari Guru aapne, Govind diyo milaaye”

Meaning: If Lord and Guru both are standing together,

Whom should I bow to first?

I first bow to my Guru,

It’s because of him that I am able to find God.

On this auspicious day of Guru Poornima, our Center devotional wing and SSIO are offering ample opportunities to remind ourselves of the glory of Guru; so, let us participate and make it an invigorating experience.

In response to a renewed appeal from OFSS for monetary donations, since they are closed due to COVID-19 and we cannot physically go there to perform seva, brother Anshuman informed last week about a seva opportunity of contributing donations for their benefit. Swami says service to man is service to God; and Guru Poornima is a very opportune time to fulfill those aspirations. If you would like to participate, please consider making donation by clicking "Read More" on this seva in Announcements/Service section.

Once again, May this Guru Poornima help us follow and put Swami’s teachings into practice to realize the true blessings of Guru Purnima.

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
