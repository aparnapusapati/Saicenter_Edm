From President's Pen ...

Sai Ram!

This coming weekend is a very special long weekend at our Centre. Besides it is Canada Day on Saturday, July 1st, and many of us will be watching the fireworks display by the City of Edmonton, a very important day falls on Monday, July 3rd: it is Guru Poornima, the auspicious day celebrated in the honor of the great Sage Vyasa, author of the greatest of epics of all times – Mahabharata and the Bhagavad Geeta, who is believed to have been born on this day. More importantly, he gathered all the Vedic hymns extant during his times and divided them into four parts based on their characteristics and use in rites, which ultimately formed four famous Vedas, namely Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva. In the Hindu yogic tradition, it is believed that on this day, Lord Shiva became the first guru. Therefore, reciting the glory of Lord Shiva is considered very auspicious. Through the chanting of Sri Rudram, Lord Shiva’s various attributes, and aspects are invoked and worshipped and are considered to be of great benefit. July 1st being a part of the long weekend, the Centre is arranging a special homam and chanting of Sri Rudram, 11 times (Ekadasha Rudram), this Saturday followed by Guru Poornima celebrations on Monday, July 3rd at 5pm.

Dear Swami has spoken a great deal about the importance of Guru Poornima: Swami revealed that “At Guru Poornima, the full moon represents the mind when it is filled with love. On the occasion of this holy festival, let us reflect on, with gratitude, those who have transformed us with this love—our parents, teachers, and those who have been an example of illumined living and self-mastery”. So, please look for the details of these festive celebrations over the long weekend, and take advantage of the rare opportunity to participate in the Ekadasha Sri Rudram Chanting and Guru Poornima.

Regarding our Western Sai Retreat in August this year, if you have already registered, you must be anxious to know further details about the retreat program and activities. We plan to refine and confirm the details in the next few days once we cross the last date to register which is this Friday, June 30th, and we know how many members are attending. In the meanwhile, please keep the bottle drive alive, and get rid of the empties in your garage besides helping in this great service initiative.

The Centre is also looking for some technically motivated volunteers to help with our computers and sound system needs at the Centre. If you are able to spare a couple of hours a week, please contact brother Troy or sister Aarti Mangru (troymangru@gamil.com).

Finally, please join me in our continued prayers for World peace.

With loving Sairam,

Prem Kharbanda
